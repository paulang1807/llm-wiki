!!! tip "EDA Tips"
    - Larger difference between the mean and the median (50% value) indicates the presence of **outliers** in the data
        - The standard deviation is also higher in such cases

## Handling Outliers
- May be a result of valid internal or external factors 
- May result from errors
- **Always analyze the outlier carefully and understand the factors that may have led to their creation**
    - Decide whether the outlier should be removed or transformed

!!! info "Outlier Treatment Approaches"
    - **[Capping and Flooring](https://colab.research.google.com/drive/1d2YwcvmaJpb8EfyuEPT4eqZdhxMH7wkT#scrollTo=9bcd3496-f946-4b12-a59b-826ba328803e)**
        - Impute values above 99th percentile ($P99$) and below 1st percentile ($P1$)

            - Use a multiplier $x$ to determine which values above $P99$ and below $P1$ to impute

                - $x = 1$ canbe used to impute all values above $P99$ and below $P1$

                - $x = 2$ canbe used to impute all values above $2 * P99$ and below $P1 / 2$

            - Replace values greater then $x * P99$ with $x * P99$ and values less than $P1 / x$ with $P1 / x$ respectively

    - **[Exponential Smoothing](https://colab.research.google.com/drive/1d2YwcvmaJpb8EfyuEPT4eqZdhxMH7wkT#scrollTo=fca5aa42-da25-406e-a780-3860e24a05a9)**
        - Mostly applied when the bivariate plot is logarithmic
        - Extrapolate the curve between $P5$ and $P1$ and between $P95$ and $P99$

            - Cap all values falling outside these to the value generated by the curve
        - Not related to [Time Series Exponential Smoothing](../stats-tsb/#cust-id-tsb-exp-smth)

    - **Sigma Approach**
        - Identify outliers as the values falling outside $\mu \pm x \sigma$ where $\mu$ is the mean and $\sigma$ is the standard deviation

            - Replace them with $\mu \pm x \sigma$

            - Similar to the Capping and Flooring approach

## Handling Multicollinearity
- If the [correlation matrix](https://colab.research.google.com/drive/1d2YwcvmaJpb8EfyuEPT4eqZdhxMH7wkT#scrollTo=R3dy0pvs3-5e) shows high correlation between two variables, we should remove one of the variables to avoid [multicollinearity](../stats-reg/#cust-id-stats-reg-multicol)
    - Remove the variable that is functionally less important
    - **If both are important, keep the one with higher correlation with the dependent variable**
- We need to encode the categorical variables before generating the correlation matrix
